How it works

🌱 What is Commit Garden?
A habit tracker for developers. Every time you commit code anywhere on this machine, your plant grows. Miss a day and it wilts.
💧 How to water your plant
Type what you learned today and click Commit. It writes to your log, runs Git, updates the plant SVG, and pushes everything to GitHub automatically.
🔥 Streak rules
Commit once per day to keep your streak alive. Committing multiple times in one day counts as one. Missing a day resets your streak to 1 and your plant wilts.

Your streak is shown in the top bar as 🔥 Xd. Best streak is tracked separately so you always know your record.
⚡ Daily commit bonus
Committing more than once in a day unlocks a bonus badge on your plant panel:

2 commits → ✨ Double tap!
3–4 commits → ⚡ Power Day!
5+ commits → 🔥 On Fire!

The badge resets the next day.
🌿 Plant unlocks
Day 1–5   → Sprout 🌱
Day 6–10  → Cactus 🌵
Day 11–20 → Tree 🌳
Day 21–29 → Cherry Blossom 🌸
Day 30+   → Sunflower 🌻

Each plant is shown in the Plants page with days remaining to unlock.
🪝 Git hook
A post-commit Git hook runs automatically after every commit inside this repo, updating your garden behind the scenes.
📁 Repository
commit-garden — all data lives in the data/ folder. The SVG updates on every commit and is shown live in your GitHub README.
